Breath.. It..

Breathing Practices are a structured set of sessions where conscious, directed breathing becomes the key to transforming physiological, emotional, and mental states. In the EnergyBreathing method, breathing is not just a tool for relaxation but an active engine of transformation.

These practices includes:
  • Alternation of breathing phases: active phases (accelerated or intensified breathing) and slowed or held-breath phases, which allow recovery and integration.
  • Audio stimulation: music, soundscapes, and rhythmic bpm frequencies synchronized with the breath enhance the effect — helping the brain switch more easily between sympathetic and parasympathetic modes.
  • Neurophysiological impact: breathing influences baroreceptors, CO₂/O₂ balance, and the vestibular system. Moderate hypoxia/hypercapnia, as seen in Wim Hof and resistance breathing practices, increases stress resilience, boosts dopamine/serotonin release, and enhances neuroplasticity.
  • Controlled duration and frequency: sessions can last from just a few minutes to two hours, with adjustable cycles and phases. This allows tailoring to individual resources, conditions, and states.
